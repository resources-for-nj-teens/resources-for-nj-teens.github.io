About

This website provides a variety of direct links to online resources like educational aid, standardized exam practice, and important life skills information that can aid students throughout their journey through high school, college, and adult life.

Scholarship Resources


Quest Bridge

Low income, academically excelling students can get paired with colleges around the world to get scholarships and financial aid.

College Scholarships.org

A list of college scholarships from various organizations. Narrow down the list by major, ethnicity, state, etc. and apply for the scholarships relevant to you!

School Resources


Khan Academy

Online articles and videos to help with various classes.

College Back Tests

Link of free (no login required) back tests (previous years’ exams) from university classes at JHU, UPenn, Drexel, Penn State, and Univ of Maryland-- useful to use as practice tests!

Anki

Flash card app! Really useful for studying, especially on the go. Free on Android phones.

PatrickJMT

An excellent source for step-by-step math tutorial videos when you’re struggling to understand a specific concept. Videos cover the following topics: algebra, arithmetic, calculus, differential equations, discrete math, linear algebra, probability and statistics, trigonometry, physics, and more.

Life Skills Resources


How to Open a Bank Account

It’s never too early to start saving! Step-by-step instructions on opening a bank account. You must be 18 years or older to open a bank account on your own. If you are under 18 and over 13 years old, you can open a bank account with a parent/guardian.

How to Write a Check

An in-depth guide to writing a check.

A career quiz from the Princeton Review that lets teenagers know what career will best fit their interests/personalities.

A PDF guide for youth and/or their caregivers who are interested in joining the military.
